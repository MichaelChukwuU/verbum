YOUR COMPUTER DESERVES A LONG LIFE TOO!

The best way to care for your computer is to provide it with the best care possible. Basic software and hardware maintenance is easy to perform and also extends the life of your computer.

Below are some of the cares you should accord your computer to ensure it a long life;

INSTALL AN ANTIVIRUS SOFTWARE

Antivirus software is responsible for catching viruses in spammy downloads and fishy websites. Some programs are free, while other carry price tags. Even if you choose to pay for the software, it�s still far less than what it would cost to have to restore your system if it gets corrupted. It is therefore a pre requisite to ensure the safety of your computer. Know this and know safety.

PERFORM REGULAR SOFTWARE UPDATES

Software updates keep your computer running very smoothly. Updating software on your computer generally helps to fix bugs and malfunctions as well as enhances the security features on your computer.

�BACKUP YOUR FILES

It�s always a good idea to backup all files and you can never go wrong with backups. This is important so that the files are still available if something happens to your computer. External hard drives are the popular choice for backing up photos, videos and other files. Software-based backups are also useful and allow you to go back in your computer�s timeline in case of a malfunction.

�KEEP YOUR KEYBOARD CRUMB FREE

It�s common to eat over your keyboard, but it is a habit you should stop. Crumbs are more than a nuisance; they can damage the internal parts of the keyboard. Not eating in the computer room or over your computer was one of the earliest precautions of a computer we knew when we were younger.

CLEAN YOUR SCREEN ALWAYS

Another part of your computer that you should keep clean is the screen. Follow the manufacturer�s recommendations for cleaning, and always use a soft cloth or wipes specifically made for electronics. Avoid leaving dust or fragments on your computer screen. Once your screen is bad, its as good as saying your computer is bad and you do not want that to happen.

REMOVE DUST FROM VENTS AND FANS

The fan and vents keep your computer from getting too hot, but dust can get inside and cause them to run slow. Luckily, you can either clean the fan and vents yourself or take your PC to a computer shop.

�AVOID EXTREME TEMPERATURES

Moderate temperatures of 68 to 71 degrees Fahrenheit are ideal temperatures for computers. Avoid leaving your laptop in too hot or cold places; damp basement for instance.

CARRY LAPTOPS IN CASES

If you bring your computer with you to school or work, make sure that you transport it in a padded carrying case or bag. These cases not only protect computers from scratches and dents, but also, they prevent them from getting dust and debris inside the fans, vents, etc.

Take care of your computer and it will reward you with a long life!�
